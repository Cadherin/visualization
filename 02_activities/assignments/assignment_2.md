    - Explain (with reference to material covered up to date, along with readings and other scholarly sources, as needed) why you classified that visualization the way you did.
      
      ```
      The two pieces of data visualizations selected for review were sourced from the following:
      
      1. The good: Ukraine’s refugee crisis is unprecedented. The response must be, too.
      https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2022/03/15/opinion/ukraine-refugee-crisis.html 
      
      Note: The visualizations being reviewed include the visualization pair that work together to depict the differences between the number of refugees from all countries in Eastern Europe in June 2021 vs. February 2026. It is the second set of visualization from the top in this article. Data is sourced from the UN Refugee Agency (UNHCR). 
      
      Commentary: This article was an opinion piece from the New York Times that reviewed the number of Ukrainian residents who have left the country since Russia’s invasion. The headline was that almost 3 million of Ukraine’s 44 million residents left the country. The visualization pair reviewed illustrated where the 3-million residents migrated to as refugees within a short timespan. This is an example of a good visualization based on the following qualities used for my assessment:
      
        a. Aesthetic (is the visualization pleasing to look at): The visualization features a light-color minimalistic map background that illustrates selected east European countries. The only color legend used are purple dots, each representing 1,000 refugees. The graphics are simple, clear and vivid.
        
        b. Substantive (does the visualization accurately and honestly present data): The two sets of maps present the number of refugees migrated from Ukraine to other east European countries using the following visualization techniques:
            
            i. Spatial: geographical patterns of the migration
            
            ii. Change over time (temporal): Difference of the number of refugees in two points in time (i.e. Before Russian invasion in June 2021 and after Russian invasion in February 2022)
            
            iii.	Flow: Intensity or volume of movement between locations and two points in time.
            
            iv.	Magnitude: The dot density chart on top of the map 
        
        c. Perceptual (Can we understand what message the maker of the visualization is attempting to convey): The message is clear that many Ukrainians left the country shortly after Russian’s invasion. The map clearly illustrates where they have moved as well as the top countries where they now reside including Poland, Hungary and Slovakia.

      2. The not so good: An article published by the New York Times titled “The Global AI Divide”. It can be viewed here: https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/06/23/technology/ai-computing-global-divide.html
      
      Note: The visualization being reviewed is related to a map view of global data center locations at the top of the article linked above. This data visualization was reproduced by the New York Times using data published by the Oxford University in a publication titled “Compute North Vs. Compute South: The Uneven Possibilities of Compute-Based AI Governance Around the Globe”. The publication is located here: 
      https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/8yp7z_v1 
      
      Commentary: This article was an opinion piece from the New York Times that discussed The Global AI Divide whereby only 32 nations and mostly those in the Northern hemisphere have AI specialized data centres. This is an example of a mediocre data visualization with some misses based on the following qualities used in my assessment: 
      
        a. Aesthetic (is the visualization pleasing to look at): The visualization features a light-color minimalistic map background that illustrates selected east European countries. The only color legend used are blue dots. There is however not a legend that clearly illustrates what each blue dot represents. For instance, does each dot represent one AI specialized data centre only or more?
        
        b. Substantive (does the visualization accurately and honestly present data): The map only presents where the AI specialized data centres are located around the world at a high level, the message that the article attempted to convey is not quite coming out of the visualization:
        
            i. Spatial: The visualization shows the geographical distribution of AI specialized data centres as dots with a single-color legend on the map. However, if the headline of the article is The Global AI Divide and that the authors would like to make a point about the North vs. the South, then the visualization should focus on illustrating this point.
            
            ii. Magnitude: The difference on the magnitude of AI specialized data centres between the North and the South is up for interpretation. The visualization only shows where the data centres are and not more. 
        
        c. Perceptual (Can we understand what message the maker of the visualization is attempting to convey): The message is not clear on the total number of AI specialized data centres in the North vs. South without supplementary visualizations or reading details in the article. This is up for interpretation using solely the global map visualization.

    - How could this data visualization have been improved?

      ```
      The following enhancements could make the map better:
      
      1. It would make sense to enhance the Global AI Divide visualization to have a two-color legend: For the dots located in the Northern hemisphere to be colored differently from those in the Southern hemisphere. This would provide a stark contrast between the North and the South. Otherwise, it is up for interpretation.
      
      2. There should be a legend that clearly labels what each dot means on the map.
      
      3. In addition to the global map that illustrates the distribution of the AI specialized data centres, there could be a supplementary table next to it provides the count of AI specialized data centres in each country featured. This would mitigate the cognitive load from the readers on dot counting.
