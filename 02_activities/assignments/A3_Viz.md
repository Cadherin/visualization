# Polllinator gardens established under the PollinaTO program between 2020 and 2025

PollinateTO is a program offered by the City of Toronto that provides grants of up to $5,000 to support community-led projects that:
- Create a new pollinator garden or rain garden
- Expand or enhance an existing garden by adding native pollinator-friendly plants
- Convert a lawn area, boulevard or hard surface to a pollinator garden

Using dataset available on the City of Toronto open data catalogue, the number of cumulative pollinator gardens created under this program across the city are shown in the choropleth map below. City wards including Parkdale-High Park (23), Toronto Danforth (19), Toronto Centre (16) and Davenport (16) are city wards that have benefited the most from this program. They have the respective number of pollinator gardens shown in parenthese established between 2020 and 2025.

A breakdown of these gardens by type and funded year are shown in the horizontal stacked chart below. We can see that school learning gardens make up the majority pollinator gardens since the inception of the program, followed by city parks and communal gardens. Right-of-way, multi-residential and multi-boulevard gardens make up the lowest number of pollinator gardens under this program.
